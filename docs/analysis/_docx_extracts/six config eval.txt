Key Takeaways — Anti-UAV
IMPORTANT
RGB dominates on Anti-UAV. F1=0.99 is near-perfect. The RGB model almost never misses a drone (FN=639 out of ~80,000 positives) and fires very few false alarms (FP=921).
RGB is the star: F1=0.9902 (IoU) / 0.9925 (IoP). Nearly perfect.
IR is slightly weaker on recall: F1=0.9619. It misses 4,818 drone instances that RGB catches. This is likely due to thermal signature ambiguity (drone blends into warm backgrounds, or small drones lack contrast).
Classifier (meta-model) averages between them: F1=0.9774. It pools detections from both modalities, so it inherits IR's misses as FN and IR's extra FPs. It doesn't beat RGB alone because RGB is already so strong here.
Filter has near-zero impact: On Anti-UAV there are no confusers to filter. The filter removes 1–4 TPs and 1–4 FPs — statistically irrelevant. This proves the filter is safe — it doesn't randomly destroy detections when there are no confusers.
IoU vs IoP gap is small: Anti-UAV annotations are tight and well-aligned, so both matching strategies agree.

Explanation of Key Phenomena
Why Does "Drone" Appear as an FP Category?
This is the most confusing part of the results. A "DRONE" FP does not mean the model detected a non-drone and called it a drone. It means:
The frame does contain a drone, the model did detect something, but the detection box and the ground-truth box don't overlap enough (IoU < 0.5). The detection is scored as FP, and the unmatched GT is scored as FN.
Proof: Under IoP matching, RGB's "DRONE" FPs collapse from 6,905 → 72 (a 99% drop). The detections were correct the whole time — they were tight predictions inside Svanström's giant, padded GT boxes. IoU punishes this; IoP forgives it.
Why Is RGB Terrible on Svanström?
Two reasons stacking on top of each other:
Confusers: RGB correctly detects airplanes (3,315 FPs), birds (2,418), and helicopters (4,971) — it doesn't know these aren't drones. IR is nearly immune to these because its training data didn't include these confusers and their thermal signatures are different.
Loose GT boxes (IoU only): On top of the confuser problem, the tight RGB predictions inside oversized GT boxes get marked as FP. Switching to IoP fixes this, and RGB F1 jumps from 0.08 → 0.53.
The Classifier Is Remarkably Effective
Under IoP matching on Svanström:
Precision = 0.989 — only 207 FPs across 28,710 frames
It achieves this by routing decisions through the modality it trusts. Since IR is reliable on Svanström (no airplanes/helis in IR), the classifier learns to rely on IR and reject RGB-only detections
The FP breakdown confirms this: classifier produces zero airplane and helicopter FPs (RGB's biggest weakness), and only 19 bird FPs (IR's minor weakness).
The Filter's Value Is Modality-Specific
On raw RGB (the main use case for the filter):
TIP
The filter crushed 76% of all RGB false positives with only a moderate recall cost (69% → 65%). Helicopters and airplanes were almost entirely eliminated.
On the classifier: The filter is redundant. The classifier already achieves near-zero confuser FPs via modality routing. Adding the filter slightly hurts recall (0.817 → 0.785) without improving precision. On Svanström, the classifier alone is sufficient.
On IR: The filter has minimal impact — IR had almost no confuser FPs to begin with (117 bird FPs → 73, that's it).
On Anti-UAV: The filter is dormant — no confusers exist in the dataset, so it changes nothing (±1-4 detections).

Summary Table — F1 Scores Across Everything

Thesis-Ready Conclusions
Single-modality performance is domain-dependent: RGB excels on Anti-UAV (F1=0.99) but collapses on Svanström (F1=0.08 IoU / 0.53 IoP) due to confusers. IR is consistent across both (~0.95-0.96). This proves the need for multi-modal fusion.
The meta-classifier is the best general-purpose strategy: On Anti-UAV it achieves F1=0.98 (near RGB). On Svanström it achieves F1=0.89 (IoP) — massively outperforming rgb_only while maintaining IR's confuser immunity. It does this with only 207 total FPs across 28,710 frames.
The confuser filter is a targeted RGB remedy, not a universal one: It cuts RGB FPs by 76% on Svanström (the confuser-heavy dataset) with no measurable cost on Anti-UAV (the confuser-free dataset). It is complementary to raw single-modality inference, but redundant when the classifier is already routing decisions.
IoP is the honest metric for Svanström: The IoU-vs-IoP gap on DRONE-category FPs (6,905 → 72 for RGB) proves that annotation quality, not model quality, drives the IoU scores on this dataset.
CAUTION
Caveat on IR's Svanström numbers: The IR model saw Svanström data during training. Its high F1 (0.96) partially reflects memorization, not pure generalization. Anti-UAV is the unbiased benchmark for IR.

The Big Picture
Anti-UAV (85K frames, clean benchmark):
RGB is king: F1 = 0.99. Near-perfect.
IR is strong but slightly behind: F1 = 0.96 (misses ~4,800 drones RGB catches).
Classifier averages them: F1 = 0.98. Can't beat RGB alone here because RGB is already so good.
Filter does nothing — no confusers exist in this dataset. But crucially, it doesn't hurt either (±1-4 detections out of 80,000).
Svanström (28K frames, confuser stress-test, IoP metric):
IR stays strong: F1 = 0.96 (but caveat: training overlap).
RGB collapses: F1 = 0.53. Gets fooled by airplanes (3,315 FPs), helicopters (4,971 FPs), birds (2,418 FPs).
Classifier is the hero: F1 = 0.89, Precision = 0.989. Only 207 FPs across 28,710 frames. It learns to trust IR and reject RGB-only hallucinations.
Filter rescues raw RGB: cuts FPs by 76% (10,776 → 2,568). Helicopters −89%, airplanes −80%.
The Three Thesis Takeaways
No single modality works everywhere — RGB fails on confusers, IR fails on some drone poses. This motivates your fusion architecture.
The classifier is the best general strategy — 0.98 on Anti-UAV, 0.89 on Svanström with near-perfect precision.
The filter is complementary insurance — safe (doesn't hurt on clean data), powerful on RGB confusers (−76% FPs), redundant when the classifier is already routing. Its real value is the "correlated failure" scenario (both modalities fooled) where the classifier is blind.
