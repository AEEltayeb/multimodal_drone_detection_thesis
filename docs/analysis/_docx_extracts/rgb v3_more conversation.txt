  eval_rgb_finetune.py:76 → RGB_CONF = 0.25 (constant). Both   
  OLD and v3_more were evaluated at exactly this threshold —

  Why this matters

  Old and v3_more have different P/R curves and therefore      
  different F1-optimal conf thresholds. Common pattern after   
  hard-negative finetuning:
  - v3_more shifts its confidence distribution down (more      
  confusers in the 0.25–0.30 band that the original at 0.30+   
  would suppress entirely)
  - v3_more's F1-optimal conf is probably higher than old's —  
  maybe 0.30 or 0.35 — because at 0.25 it admits more low-conf 
  confuser boxes than old did

  What this implies for your numbers

  The "−0.7pt drone F1 on antiuav, −2.2pt on dataset_rgb"      
  deltas were measured at conf=0.25 for both. If you
  re-evaluated each model at its own F1-optimal threshold:     
  - old's optimum is probably close to 0.25 already (it was    
  trained without hard negatives, so its conf distribution is  
  well-calibrated for plain detection)
  - v3_more's optimum is probably 0.30–0.35
  - The drone F1 gap would likely shrink or possibly flip      

  The svanstrom confuser-suppression win (−15pt any-det rate)  
  was measured at 0.30 in the OTHER eval
  (compare_old_new_rgb.py) and at 0.25 in eval_rgb_finetune.py.   v3_more wins at both.

  How to find the optimal

  classifier/sweep_all_thresholds.py. Either should sweep conf 
  for v3_more. Then you can report:
  - "v3_more at its F1-optimal conf (e.g. 0.32) matches old's  
  drone F1 within X pt while still cutting confuser any-det by 
  Y pt."

  That's a stronger thesis claim than "v3_more loses 1pt F1 at 
  a fixed conf=0.25." Want me to check what sweep_*.py does and   propose a targeted sweep?

  For deployment though, conf=0.25 is fine — the downstream    
  classifier+filter chain mops up the extra low-conf boxes     
  (eval_six_configs already showed classifier_filter zeroes out   AIRPLANE/HELI FPs in both pipelines at 0.25).
