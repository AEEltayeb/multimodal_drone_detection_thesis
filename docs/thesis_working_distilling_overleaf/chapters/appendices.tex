\chapter{Datasets in Detail}
\label{app:datasets}

This appendix consolidates the per-source / per-clip dataset metadata that the methodology chapter summarizes. Numbers come from the dataset YAMLs (\texttt{rgb\_dataset/\allowbreak dataset.yaml}, \texttt{IR\_dset\_final/\allowbreak dataset.yaml}), the documentation file shipped with the confuser dataset (\texttt{rgb\_confusers\_merged/\allowbreak dataset\_documentation.md}), the Svanstr\"om paired-dataset meta file, and the YouTube extraction manifests.

\section{Composite RGB Training Dataset}

The full distribution by source prefix is given in Table~\ref{tab:ds_rgb_components}; the split policy is 80 / 10 / 10 with sequence-disjoint partitioning where the source did not already supply its own splits. Six negative-source rows (VIRAT, UA-DETRAC, BDD100K) contribute the 21.9\% background-negative fraction. The AirBird subset (10{,}000 images) is the bird hard-negative channel the baseline detector relies on; the composite dataset does not contain Svanstr\"om bird / airplane / helicopter splits, which are therefore OOD at evaluation time.
% [source: dataset=rgb_dataset; file=rgb_dataset/dataset.yaml]

\section{Confuser Hard-Negative Dataset}

Per-source composition is in Table~\ref{tab:ds_confusers}. Split policy:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Sources with existing splits} (Airplane Roboflow, New\_Dataset Roboflow): original train / valid / test partitions preserved.
    \item \textbf{Sources without splits}: split by video / sequence ID at 80 / 10 / 10 to prevent leakage.
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Svanstr\"om: 165 video sequences split by sequence ID.
        \item raihanrsd: 58 video folders split by folder.
        \item Helicopter Kaggle: 20 hash-based pseudo-groups.
    \end{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Mislabeled frame removal}: 10 frames in the airplane / New\_Dataset sources contained visible drones and were excised before training.
\end{itemize}

\noindent The mined-hard-negative process for \texttt{retrained\_v2} ran the baseline detector on every confuser image and kept the 11{,}729 frames (43.4\%) on which the baseline fired; those mined frames were merged with the composite RGB dataset to produce the final 183{,}751-image \texttt{retrain\_dataset}. \texttt{hardneg\_v3more} differs in using a smaller, less aggressive merge.
% [source: dataset=rgb_confusers_merged; file=rgb_confusers_merged/dataset_documentation.md]

\section{IR Detector Dataset}

The per-prefix distribution is in Table~\ref{tab:ds_ir_components}. The Svanstr\"om IR partition (\texttt{svan}, 21{,}637 frames across the dataset' train/val/test splits: 17{,}314 train, 2{,}050 val, 2{,}273 test, plus 1{,}325 zoom-augmented copies under \texttt{czoom}) is the largest single Svanstr\"om appearance in any training dataset in this thesis and is the reason Svanstr\"om IR evaluation is in-distribution for the IR detector (Table~\ref{tab:svanstrom_audit}).
% [source: ls G:/drone/IR_dset_final/{train,val,test}/images svan_* counts 17314/2050/2273; czoom_svan_* 1325; thesis_eval/results/leakage_controlled.json derived] The dataset state documented here is the state at the \texttt{Final} checkpoint; the V2--V6 revision history (Section~\ref{sec:ir_evolution}) operated on smaller predecessor variants of the same source families.

\section{Thermal Confuser Dataset (\texttt{IR\_confusers})}

5{,}938 thermal frames containing aerial confusers and no drones: 4{,}281 airplane, 1{,}200 bird, and 457 helicopter frames (category encoded in the filename prefix), exported from Roboflow-hosted thermal confuser video; source streams disjoint from every \texttt{ir\_dset\_final} source family. The evaluated split holds 5{,}237 frames, sampled to 4{,}000 by even spread (3{,}043 airplane / 871 bird / 86 helicopter). All frames carry empty YOLO labels; every detection on this surface is a false positive by construction. The dataset contributes no training data to any model; it is the OOD stress surface for the IR branch (Section~\ref{sec:ds_ir_confusers}). The thermal-native head is trained with the GT-aware CBAM \emph{train} split; its held-out gates are the disjoint \emph{CBAM valid} split (drone recall recovered $0.717 \to 0.967$) and the held-out \texttt{IR\_confusers} val/test re-mine ($90 \to 22$ residual fires, $94\%$ removed; Section~\ref{sec:ir_xmodal_verifier}).

\section{Svanstr\"om Paired}

28{,}710 frames, sampled at stride~3 from 279 source video sequences. Per-class instance counts: drone 11{,}695; airplane 6{,}090; bird 5{,}298; helicopter 5{,}627. Native resolution $640{\times}512$ (RGB) with a co-registered IR channel at the same resolution. Annotators drew loose boxes around small drones, motivating the IoP@0.5 scoring rule of Section~\ref{sec:metrics}. The dataset ships as source videos with MATLAB \texttt{groundTruth} annotation objects~\cite{svanstrom2022dronedataset}; the frames and YOLO labels used here were produced by this project's own conversion script, so box geometry may differ marginally from the authors' MATLAB pipeline, and the original videos are retained alongside the converted dataset.
% [source: github.com/DroneDetectionThesis/Drone-detection-dataset; conversion = project script; originals at G:/drone/svanstrom_paired]

\section{Anti-UAV RGBT}

The converted YOLO test + val splits used for evaluation comprise 85{,}374 paired frames at $1920{\times}1080$ (RGB) and $640{\times}512$ (IR). Anti-UAV is a tracking benchmark in its native form; the per-frame detection evaluation reported in this thesis is computed by re-scoring the tracking benchmark's frame-level GT boxes with the IoU@0.5 matching rule.

\section{DUT Anti-UAV}

The DUT Anti-UAV detection dataset~\cite{zhao2022dutantiuav} ships $10{,}000$ aerial RGB images with author-provided train / validation / test partitions. It enters this thesis twice. As a \emph{training} source, $5{,}200$ of its images contribute to the composite RGB dataset (the \texttt{dut} prefix in Table~\ref{tab:ds_rgb_components}), so the production RGB detector \texttt{ft4} is in-distribution on it. As an \emph{evaluation} surface, the official test split (the $2{,}200$ frames / $2{,}245$ ground-truth drones used in Table~\ref{tab:ablation_dut}) is the confuser-free, all-drone RGB check of Section~\ref{sec:pipeline_paired}, evaluated at \texttt{imgsz=960} with the thermal detector in its grayscale-fallback mode. No DUT frame used at evaluation time was a training image for the trust router or the per-frame filters.
% [source: github.com/wangdongdut/DUT-Anti-UAV; cite=zhao2022dutantiuav; eval split = thesis_eval/results_dut/tier1_results.json]

\section{SelCom CCTV}

Single-camera source: fixed perimeter installation, $1920{\times}1080$, 25~fps. Source clip $\sim$1:30, 2{,}076 extracted frames (1{,}953 positive + 123 explicit negative). Median drone $\sqrt{\text{area}} \approx 36.8$~px in the native frame (so $\sim$12~px at \texttt{imgsz=640}, $\sim$24~px at \texttt{imgsz=1280}). The 80 / 20 train / val split (20\% pure-SelCom validation, seed~0) is preserved across the \texttt{ft1} / \texttt{mixed\_ft1} / \texttt{mixed\_ft2} / \texttt{mixed\_ft2\_1280} variants; the strongest CCTV-evaluated fine-tune is \texttt{Yolo26n\_selcom\_mixed\_ft2\_1280}, a comparison detector; the production RGB detector is \texttt{ft4} (Section~\ref{sec:production_stack}).
% [source: dataset=selcom_cctv; file=SelCom CCTV manifest (see Section~\ref{sec:ds_selcom})]

\section{Licensing and Ethics}

The public benchmark datasets (Anti-UAV, Svanstr\"om, the Roboflow community sets) are used under their published research licenses; the IR component sources (FLIR, DV5, CST, etc.) are similarly research-license open. The SelCom CCTV stream is deployment-partner proprietary and is not redistributable; only summary metrics and qualitative figures derived from it appear in this thesis. The YouTube clips are short, low-resolution excerpts used under fair-use for research and contain no personally identifying information; the encoded source IDs in the clip filenames are preserved so the originals can be located by an interested party. None of the datasets include human faces or location markers beyond what is visible in the original public datasets.

\chapter{Number Provenance and Reproducibility}
\label{app:provenance}

Every headline number in this thesis is the value of a named cell in a canonical results file, and every such file is reproducible by a stated command. The map lives in the project repository as \texttt{runs/README.md}; this appendix reproduces it. Three layers make the chain auditable:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Unified detection cache.} \texttt{thesis\_eval/pipeline\_cache\_unified.py} runs the production detectors \emph{once} per evaluation surface and stores, per frame, every detection (box, confidence, the 517-D feature vector, patch-classifier probability), the trust classifier's input features, and the per-modality ground truth. Caches are tagged with the detector weight identity.
    \item \textbf{Deterministic replays.} Every evaluation in Chapter~\ref{ch:hitl} is a CPU re-score of that cache (no detector forward pass), so any reader with the repository can re-derive any table in about a minute per surface. The replay scripts and their outputs are listed in Table~\ref{tab:provenance}.
    \item \textbf{Automated audit.} \texttt{thesis\_eval/\_audit\_headline\_numbers.py} asserts, cell by cell, that the numbers printed in this document equal the values in the canonical results files; the submission build of this thesis passes that audit.
\end{enumerate}

\chapter{Glossary of Abbreviations and Shorthand}
\label{app:glossary}

\begin{description}
    \item[C-UAS] Counter-Unmanned Aerial Systems. The defensive use-case context for this thesis.
    \item[UAV] Unmanned Aerial Vehicle, used interchangeably with ``drone'' for consumer-class quadcopters in this thesis.
    \item[YOLO] You Only Look Once. The single-stage object-detection family~\cite{redmon2016yolo}; this thesis uses YOLO26n via the Ultralytics framework~\cite{ultralytics2024}.
    \item[CNN] Convolutional Neural Network. The patch filter is a MobileNetV3-class CNN~\cite{howard2019mobilenetv3}.
    \item[HITL] Human-in-the-Loop. The dataset / model co-development protocol of Section~\ref{sec:hitl_ir}.
    \item[OOD] Out-Of-Distribution. Imagery drawn from a different population than the model's training distribution.
    \item[FPPI] False Positives Per Image. The per-frame false-alarm count, used as the primary metric on confuser-only evaluation surfaces.
    \item[FPR] False Positive Rate. In this thesis: the frame-level fraction of confuser-only frames with at least one detection.
    \item[IoU] Intersection over Union. The standard detection-matching rule; used for Anti-UAV scoring.
    \item[IoP] Intersection over Prediction. The matching rule used for Svanstr\"om RGB scoring (Section~\ref{sec:metrics}); $\text{IoP} = |B_\text{pred} \cap B_\text{gt}| / |B_\text{pred}|$.
    \item[ft4] \texttt{Yolo26n\_selcom\_confuser\_ft4\_1280}. The production RGB detector (SelCom$+$confuser fine-tune); the only confuser-injection config that passed every drone-recall regression gate (Section~\ref{sec:training_recipes}).
    \item[robust6] The six-feature trust classifier (per-modality detection confidence $+$ box geometry), its features screened by the scene-fingerprint ratio (Section~\ref{sec:feature_selection}); the base that \texttt{robust8} extends.
    \item[robust8] The four-class trust classifier (Section~\ref{sec:feature_selection}): \texttt{robust6} plus two further free features and a tuned per-class decision threshold; retained as the reject-class ablation and recommended on confuser-rich paired streams.
    \item[robust8-nr] The shipped production trust router (Section~\ref{sec:classifier_results}): the eight \texttt{robust8} features with the \texttt{reject} class dropped, so it always routes RGB/IR/both and the per-frame filter owns false-positive rejection.
    \item[sa32] \texttt{scene\_aware\_\allowbreak v3more\_\allowbreak 32feat}. The strongest hand-engineered comparison trust classifier (32-feature XGBoost); it leaks scene statistics (discriminates \emph{which scene} a frame came from, not \emph{whether} its detections are trustworthy), so its in-domain lead is not trusted to generalize, and it is superseded in production by \texttt{robust8-nr} (Section~\ref{sec:feature_selection}).
    \item[fnfn] \texttt{fusion\_no\_fn\_v1.1}. The open-world fallback trust classifier (40-feature).
    \item[v3b] The production IR detector weights: 2-epoch corrective fine-tune on top of the \texttt{Final} IR checkpoint.
    \item[\texttt{mlp\_v5\_v4}] The production distilled feature-space confuser filter (Section~\ref{sec:distill_verifier}): an MLP on the detector's fused \texttt{p3}+\texttt{p5} features that supersedes the v2 patch filter on confuser-rich surfaces and runs per-frame; the \texttt{v4} bird-split re-mine closes the \texttt{rgb\_dataset\_test} coverage gap.
    \item[\texttt{mlp\_v5\_base}] The pre-bird-split predecessor of \texttt{mlp\_v5\_v4} (Section~\ref{sec:distill_verifier}); retained only as the held-out-bird comparison baseline.
    \item[IR filter] The thermal-native IR confuser filter (Section~\ref{sec:ir_xmodal_verifier}): a single head (\texttt{mlp\_aligned\_thermalonly}, CBAM-trained, @0.05) trained directly on thermal confuser crops.
    \item[selcom\_1280] The SelCom CCTV RGB fine-tune \texttt{Yolo26n\_\allowbreak selcom\_\allowbreak mixed\_\allowbreak ft2\_1280} evaluated at \texttt{imgsz=1280}; a comparison/ablation detector (the production RGB detector is \texttt{ft4}, Section~\ref{sec:production_stack}).
    \item[selcom\_640] The same SelCom weights evaluated at \texttt{imgsz=640} (resolution-ablation variant).
    \item[\texttt{imgsz}] Ultralytics short for input letterbox size. Drives small-object recall (Section~\ref{sec:resolution_arch}).
\end{description}

\chapter{Superseded Components and Design History}
\label{app:design_history}

This appendix collects short notes on the components and operating points that were superseded in the production pipeline, gathered here so the main text of Chapter~\ref{ch:hitl} stays focused on the shipped system.


\section{Patch Filter (Superseded Predecessor)}
\label{sec:patch_verifier_arch}

The predecessor confuser filter was a four-class image classifier (\texttt{airplane} / \texttt{helicopter} / \texttt{bird} / \texttt{other}) on a MobileNetV3-Small backbone~\cite{howard2019mobilenetv3}, run on each YOLO crop. It was trained on \textbf{45{,}917 RGB+IR patches} (ground-truth drone crops plus bird, airplane, and helicopter crops hard-negative-mined from detector false positives), and at inference emitted a per-crop softmax whose confuser probability was thresholded to veto a detection. On its own validation split it reached accuracy $0.975$, but birds were its weakest class (recall $0.712$), foreshadowing the coverage gap. Its threshold sweep set \texttt{patch\_thr}$=$0.9, catching about $63\%$ of the classifier stage's residual confuser false positives at a cost of $-4.0$~pp drone F1; every confuser bucket sat below the decisiveness bar, and on SelCom-scale crops the filter went bit-for-bit the bare detector. That cost, and the bird gap, are why it was replaced by the feature-reuse \texttt{mlp\_v5\_v4} (Section~\ref{sec:distill_verifier}), which reuses features already computed per detection and so runs per frame.
% [source: models.csv patch_v2 (train_dataset=45,917 patches); ledger=patch-version-ranking,patch-catch-below-bar; eval=patch_catch_v2_svan,v5_selcom_patch; config=svan_iop_1280_s9]

\section{Alert-Gate Cascade (superseded patch path)}
\label{sec:alert_gate}

Alert-gating was forced by the patch filter's cost: a second CNN over crop pixels was too expensive to run per frame, so the filter was consulted only when the temporal smoother was about to emit an alert. The per-frame \texttt{mlp\_v5\_v4} filter removes that cost, so it runs on every frame and the composition-order question is re-answered with it in Section~\ref{sec:pipeline_ablation}.


\section{Trust-Classifier Design History}

\paragraph{The grayscale hole, and why the reject class caused it.}
\texttt{robust6} inherits one weakness from the four-class formulation: in the grayscale-IR fallback the thermal features go uninformative, so drone-only-in-RGB frames are routed to \texttt{reject} and grayscale recall collapses. \texttt{robust8} patched this within the reject formulation, lifting Svanstr\"om-grayscale recall $0.577 \to 0.681$ at the structural cost of $7$--$10\times$ real-video-confuser fire on the grayscale slice. Dropping the reject class removes the hole at its source: a router that never rejects cannot route a grayscale drone to \texttt{reject}, so \texttt{robust8-nr} needs neither the threshold dial nor that trade.
% [source: ledger=robust8-grayscale-router; eval=svan_gray_robust8_t20_clffilter,video_confuser_robust8_t20_clffilter; run=eval/compare_routing_pipeline.py]

\paragraph{A dual-classifier extension (validated, not shipped).}
Splitting the router into a paired model and a separate grayscale model strictly dominates single \texttt{sa32} on missing-modality cases (RGB-fallback bird-confuser drone $F1$ $0.247\to1.000$) with paired-core performance unchanged. It is recorded as a validated extension rather than the shipped default; \texttt{robust8}'s two extra features close most of the same gap without a second model.
% [source: ledger=dual-classifier-v3; eval=dualclf_v3_vs_sa32; config=dual_clf_v3_surfaces]

\paragraph{Design evolution: the OOD confuser zoo (predecessor configuration).}
The first quantitative router comparison ran the predecessor cascade (baseline RGB, alert-gated patch v2) over the confuser zoo: the open-world \texttt{fusion\_no\_fn\_v1.1} suppressed the bare $52.1\%$ fire to $1.6\%$ at the classifier stage, against \texttt{sa32}'s $20.5\%$, a $13\times$ gap on this surface. The ranking did not survive real video.
% [source: ledger=cascade-confuser-collapse; eval=clfzoo_fnfn,clfzoo_sa32; run=eval/cumulative_halluc.py; config=confuser_zoo_1280 (predecessor configuration)]

\paragraph{The surface reversal.}
On real video the ranking inverted: \texttt{sa32} led segment drone $F1$ while \texttt{fusion\_no\_fn\_v1.1} accepted only about one in eight of the correct RGB drone true positives. The Svanstr\"om/zoo ordering does not select the production classifier; the operational surface does. This reversal discredited hand-ranking classifiers on single benchmarks and motivated the statistical feature selection behind \texttt{robust6}/\texttt{robust8}; even \texttt{sa32}'s real-video win is not bankable, because its scene-statistic leakage (Section~\ref{sec:feature_selection}) lets it score partly by memorizing surface identity. \texttt{fusion\_no\_fn\_v1.1} is retained as the open-world fallback for deployments where a missed drone costs less than a false alarm.
