\chapter*{Abstract}

Protecting a fixed site against consumer drones demands high recall and a low false-alarm rate at once. The single-stage detectors trained in this work are already strong on ordinary footage, so the open problem is narrow but stubborn: in scenes with aerial \emph{confusers} (birds, airplanes, helicopters) the production RGB detector fires on $30.4\%$ of an out-of-distribution confuser dataset, and the obvious fix, retraining the detector to suppress them, collapses small-drone recall ($0.961 \to 0.306$). The thesis answers this with a deployable detection \emph{system} that moves confuser discrimination off the detector and into cheap downstream stages: an RGB and a thermal detector for day and night, a per-frame trust classifier that routes between them, a per-detection confuser filter that reuses the detector's own internal features to veto the false-positive classes, and a temporal aggregator that rejects transient hallucinations, all running in real time behind an operator GUI.
% [source: thesis_eval/results/tier1_results.json (antiuav/rgb_dataset_test/rgb_confuser); ledger=retrainedv2-recall-collapse]

Because the bare detectors are already good, the pipeline must prove itself where they fail. Under one standard with bootstrap confidence intervals, the full pipeline lifts Svanstr\"om paired drone F1 from $0.742$ to $0.946$ (full surface; $0.684 \to 0.913$ on the held-out clean split, Section~\ref{sec:svanstrom_audit}) while \emph{raising} recall ($0.948 \to 0.991$); on the out-of-distribution confuser dataset it cuts frame fire from $30.4\%$ to $1.4\%$; and on the saturated Anti-UAV control it does no harm ($0.973 \to 0.984$), with an ablation isolating every stage. Two methodological contributions, reusable beyond this system, produce its components. The Model MRI is a statistics-before-training instrument that measures what a detector's feature space can already separate, then trains the confuser filters on those same embeddings (positives = drone detections, negatives = confuser detections), so a filter is built only where the separation is shown to exist. The human-in-the-loop data engine co-develops the scarce-data thermal detector with its training dataset, its discipline evidenced by a documented regression and its recovery. A third result was unplanned and stands on its own: the thermal-trained detector, run zero-shot on grayscale-converted RGB, still detects drones when no thermal camera is available, a cross-modal transfer not, to our knowledge, reported for this setup.
