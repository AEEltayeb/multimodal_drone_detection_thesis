> Statistical-evidence figures and numbers in this edition were **regenerated by
> the Model MRI tool** (`python -m mri`) from the cached feature corpus the
> **shipped** MLP V5 was actually trained on
> (`eval/results/_v5_selcom_pure_1x8/training_data.npz`, 32,931 detections — the

### Root cause of #1/#2 — two corpora conflated in the original

The original report mixes numbers from **two different training runs**:

| Corpus on disk | Total | Drones | Confusers | Role |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| `_v5_selcom_pure_1x8` | 32,931 | 19,334 | 13,597 | **the shipped model** (`mlp_v5.pt`); this edition's evidence |
| `_v5_p3p5_ft4_distill` | 35,277 | 21,501 | 13,776 | the corpus the original §2 *figures* were rendered from |

- The original §4.1 figure **32,931 / 19,334 / 13,597 matches `selcom_pure_1x8` exactly** — so §4–5 correctly describe the shipped model.
- The original §2 *text* says **35,098 / 21,501 / 13,597**, which matches **neither** corpus. Most likely a mis-merge (drones 21,501 from the figure corpus, confusers 13,597 from the shipped corpus; 21,501 + 13,597 = 35,098), or a stale earlier snapshot. Either way the original showed evidence from a corpus the deployed model was never trained on.

**Fix applied here:** regenerate all evidence from the shipped corpus so the
figures and the model agree.

## 1. The statistical evidence — regenerated on the shipped corpus

Hooked YOLO FPN layers **P3** (stride 8, 64 ch) and **P5** (stride 32, 256 ch);
ROI-pooled per detection to a 512-D embedding + 5 metadata = **517-D**. Corpus:
**32,931 detections (19,334 drones + 13,597 confusers)** from
`_v5_selcom_pure_1x8` — the corpus behind the production `mlp_v5.pt`.

### 1.1 LDA — the discriminant axis

![MRI-regenerated LDA on the shipped V5 fused features (32,931 samples). Train-set linear accuracy 95.2%.](images/mri_v5_lda_fused.png)

**Finding:** the LDA histogram shows two cleanly separated peaks; a single linear
threshold reaches **95.2% train accuracy** (original said 95.4% — confirmed within
rounding). The drone/confuser signal is strongly, nearly linearly separable in
YOLO's internal features.

### 1.2 PCA — why a trained classifier is still needed

![MRI-regenerated 2-D PCA of the fused YOLO features. Blue = drones, red = confusers.](images/mri_v5_pca_fused.png)

**Finding:** in the *unsupervised* PCA projection the classes overlap heavily
(silhouette = **0.067**, ≈ zero cluster separation). The signal exists (LDA finds
it) but lives in a discriminant subspace that variance-maximizing PCA does not
expose — hence a *supervised* classifier is required.

### 1.3 Top discriminative neurons

![MRI-regenerated activation distributions for the top discriminative features.](images/mri_v5_top_neurons.png)

**Finding:** the most discriminative single features by ANOVA F are, in order:

| Rank | Feature idx | Layer / channel / cell | F-stat |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 415 | **P5 channel 154** | **42,346** |
| 2 | 129 | P3 channel 31, cell 0 | 14,655 |
| 3 | 340 | P5 channel 79 | — (cited as "Feature 340" in original) |
| 4 | 130 | P3 channel 31, cell 1 | — |
| 5 | 374 | P5 channel 113 | — (cited as "Feature 374" in original) |
| 6 | 0 | meta:conf | 10,696 |

The two P5 neurons the original highlighted (340, 374) are genuinely in the top-5.
But the **single strongest discriminator is P5 channel 154 (F = 42,346)** — nearly
3× the strongest P3 neuron and 4× the confidence score. P3 channel 31 is also
strong, occupying ranks 2 and 4 (and reappearing at lower ranks across all four
2×2 ROI cells). The confidence metadata ranks only 6th. This refutes the original
§2.4 claim that confidence is the single highest outlier.

> **Labeling-bug note:** an earlier pass of this report mislabeled the P3 features
> as "cell=1 ch=60/61". That was a channel/cell transpose inherited from the
> original `scripts/visualize_v5_features.py::_layer_name` (which assumed a
> cell-major flatten; the ROI pool is channel-major: flat index = channel·cells +
> cell). Verified empirically and fixed in
> `mri/extract.py::FeatureSchema.column_label`.

### 1.4 Per-layer discriminative power

![MRI-regenerated per-block ANOVA F-statistic distribution (metadata / P3 / P5).](images/mri_v5_per_layer_anova.png)

**Finding (MRI-measured per-block ANOVA F):**

| Block | dim | mean F | max F |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| metadata | 5 | 3,104 | 10,696 |
| P3 | 256 | 1,090 | 14,655 |
| P5 | 256 | **2,006** | **42,346** |

On the shipped corpus **P5 is the dominant discriminative layer** — it owns both
the single largest F-stat (42,346) and the highest mean (2,006), ahead of P3
(14,655 / 1,090) and metadata (10,696 / 3,104). Both learned layers carry strong
signal, so fusing P3 + P5 is justified; but the original §2.4 claim that
*metadata/confidence* is the top outlier is wrong — P5 channel 154 is.

### 1.6 Spatial activation examples — where the neurons fire

The abstract statistics above become concrete when overlaid on real detections.
For one representative example per dataset, the MRI tool re-runs the detector and
renders the discriminative-neuron activation spatially: the detection crop, the
P3 activation (stride 8, fine spatial detail), and the P5 activation (stride 32,
semantic depth). The example chosen per dataset is the detection that maximizes
activation on the top discriminative neurons.

**Drone** (Svanström, conf = 0.72):

![Drone detection — top discriminative neuron activation. P3 binds tightly to the airframe; P5 forms a semantic blob over the object.](images/mri_v5_example_drone.png)

**Confuser** (rgb_confusers test, conf = 0.29):

![Confuser detection — top discriminative neuron activation. Activation is diffuse and fragmented rather than locked onto a centralized airframe.](images/mri_v5_example_confuser.png)

On the drone, P3 activation locks tightly onto the airframe and P5 forms a clean
semantic blob centered on the object. On the confuser, the same neurons fire in a
diffuse, fragmented pattern spread across background structure — the MLP learns to
separate exactly these two activation signatures. (These panels are rendered from
a live `--pos/--neg` scan — Svanström at imgsz=1280 + rgb_confusers — since the
spatial maps are not retained in the cached feature corpus; the top-neuron channels
are from that scan's own ANOVA ranking.)

## 2. Sections not regenerable by the MRI tool

The MRI machine images the **feature space** of one detector+corpus. The original
report's §1 (problem framing), §3 (architecture), §4.2 (held-out eval datasets),
and §5 (MLP V5 vs Patch Verifier head-to-head P/R/F1 per surface) come from
**separate evaluation runs** (`eval/eval_v4_vs_patch.py`), not the feature corpus,
and are neither confirmed nor contradicted here. To re-verify them, run the MRI
tool in full mode against live image folders:

```bash
python -m mri --yolo "RGB model/Yolo26n_selcom_confuser_ft4_1280/weights/best.pt" \
  --pos "G:/drone/svanstrom_paired/RGB/images" \
  --neg "G:/drone/rgb_confusers_merged/images/test" --train-mlp
```

(A quick-subset run of exactly this returned **"Classifier strongly recommended"**
— raw hallucination 29.6%/img, LDA 1.000, projected FP cut 98%, recall cost 1.5% —
consistent with the original's headline.)

### MRI verdict for this regeneration

> **Classifier strongly recommended — large FP cut at low recall cost.**
> _19,334 drones / 13,597 confusers; LDA separability 0.952; out-of-fold recall
> cost 1.1%; the classifier rejects **97% of confuser detections** while keeping
> **98.9%** of true drones (CV F1 0.9857). Raw per-image hallucination rate not
> measured — the input was a cached feature corpus, not image folders._

This confirms the original report's central claim: the V5 MLP works and is worth
deploying. Because the staged input is a **feature-only corpus** (no per-image
negative stream), the diagnosis decides from feature separability + the
classifier's out-of-fold behavior rather than a measured FP-per-image rate; run
against live image folders (above) to also get the hallucination rate and the
FP-reduction chart.
